Imperial Survey Summary

Dumuzi is an abandoned world whose importance lies not in its resources but in what lies buried beneath its frozen deserts and wind-scoured plateaus. Numerous archaeological expeditions have uncovered evidence that the world hosted an advanced civilization tens of thousands of years before the founding of the Third Imperium.

Although no living inhabitants remain, Dumuzi attracts a steady stream of archaeologists, historians, treasure hunters, and intelligence agents. Most expeditions operate from temporary camps and orbital support vessels.

Major Sites

  • The Shepherd's City — The largest known ruin complex, stretching nearly forty kilometers beneath the ice.
  • The Obsidian Road — A perfectly preserved black roadway whose destination remains unknown.
  • Vault Seven — A sealed underground facility protected by mechanisms that continue to function despite immense age.
  • The Silent Observatory — Ancient astronomical installation aligned with objects no longer present in local space.
  • Camp Hecate — Main Imperial archaeological base.

Historical Significance

Artifacts recovered from Dumuzi have challenged accepted theories regarding early interstellar history in the Solomani Rim. Several discoveries appear to predate known human settlement by thousands of years.

Imperial scholars continue to debate whether the ruins belong to an unknown human culture, a lost branch of humanity, or a completely alien civilization.

Current Research

  • Translation of recovered data fragments.
  • Mapping underground tunnel networks beneath the Shepherd's City.
  • Investigation of dormant machinery discovered in Vault Seven.
  • Analysis of unusual star charts recovered from the Silent Observatory.
